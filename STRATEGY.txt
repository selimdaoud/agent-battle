ARCHETYPE: Adaptive Synthesizer
────────────────────────────────
MEGA is not a momentum trader, a contrarian, or a pure risk manager. It is a
regime-aware generalist that shifts its aggression level based on what the market
is doing. In trending markets it follows momentum. In uncertain or choppy
conditions it pulls back and demands stronger conviction before committing
capital. This adaptability is its core edge, and also its primary survival
criterion.


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
PART 1 — HOW SIGNALS ARE BUILT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Every tick, each of the 15 traded pairs receives a composite signal_score in
the range [-1, +1]. This score is the weighted sum of seven sub-signals, each
normalised independently before combining.

  SIGNAL              WEIGHT    WHAT IT MEASURES
  ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  cvd_norm            0.25      Cumulative Volume Delta — net taker buy vs sell
                                pressure over the last 20 bars. Positive means
                                buyers are aggressing; negative means sellers.
                                Highest weight because order flow is the most
                                direct evidence of real intent in the market.

  funding_signal      0.22      Perpetual futures funding rate, inverted as a
                                contrarian signal. High positive funding means
                                longs are crowded and paying shorts — a bearish
                                lean. High negative funding means shorts are
                                crowded — a bullish lean. Used here to avoid
                                entering when a move is already overextended
                                in positioning terms.

  momentum_1h         0.18      Z-score of the last 1-bar return against the
                                20-bar rolling standard deviation of returns.
                                Measures whether recent price movement is
                                statistically significant or just noise. A high
                                z-score means the move is outsized relative to
                                recent history — genuine momentum.

  rsi_norm            0.12      Wilder RSI(14), normalised to [-1, +1] by
                                (rsi - 50) / 50. Acts as a mild mean-reversion
                                check — prevents buying into extreme overbought
                                conditions. Lower weight because RSI is a lagging
                                indicator that often conflicts with trend signals.

  volume_zscore       0.10      Z-score of last bar's volume against recent
                                history. High volume confirms price moves;
                                low volume suggests weak, potentially false
                                breakouts.

  fear_greed_signal   0.08      Crypto Fear & Greed Index (0–100), normalised
                                to [-1, +1] as a contrarian mid-term signal.
                                Extreme Fear (0) → +1; Extreme Greed (100) → -1.
                                Low weight because it is market-wide and updated
                                once daily — useful context, not a timing signal.

  momentum_4h         0.05      Same z-score calculation as momentum_1h but on
                                a 4-hour synthetic timeframe (every 4th close).
                                Provides a higher-timeframe momentum check to
                                avoid fighting a dominant trend.

The raw weighted sum is then multiplied by a regime multiplier and clamped to
[-1, +1]. This means the same underlying data can produce a stronger or weaker
signal depending on the market regime (see Part 2).

PART 2 — REGIME DETECTION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Before any trading decision is made, each pair is classified into one of four
regimes using its last 20 closes:

  volatile       Annualised realised volatility > 80%. The market is moving
                 violently in any direction. Signal quality degrades in this
                 environment because z-scores lose meaning when distributions
                 are fat-tailed.

  trending_up    ADX proxy > 1.1 OR SMA slope > +0.3% over 5 bars, AND slope
                 is positive. Directional momentum dominates noise. Entries
                 have higher expected value when riding an established trend.

  trending_down  Same trend detection as above, but slope is negative. The
                 market is in a sustained decline.

  ranging        No significant trending signal. Price is oscillating without
                 a clear direction. Mean-reversion setups occur here, but false
                 breakouts are common.

Regime is computed independently per pair. BTC may be trending_up while SOL
is ranging and ETH is volatile — each pair carries its own regime label in its
signal vector. MEGA uses each pair's own regime when evaluating entry and exit
thresholds for that specific pair. This eliminates cross-asset contamination
where a dominant pair's regime was previously imposing its thresholds on all
other pairs.

The sole exception is the portfolio-level cash reserve check, which uses BTC's
regime (first signal) as a proxy for overall market conditions — since cash
reserve is a whole-portfolio decision, not a per-pair one.

PART 3 — ENTRY LOGIC (BUY)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

MEGA evaluates each pair independently against that pair's own regime-adjusted
thresholds, then buys the highest signal_score pair that passes all three gates:

  Gate 1 — Signal threshold (pair's own regime):
    Base:           signal_score > 0.15
    trending_up:    signal_score > 0.21   (slightly above base — trend is
                                           confirmed, but a higher bar was added
                                           after sessions showed poor trending_up
                                           PnL at lower thresholds)
    trending_down:  signal_score > 0.25   (tighter — do not buy into a falling
                                           market without strong conviction)
    volatile:       signal_score > 0.30   (much tighter — high vol means
                                           signals are noisy and reversals are
                                           fast; preserve cash)
    ranging:        signal_score > 0.34   (tightest of all — raised from 0.31
                                           after ranging win rates fell below
                                           28% across 3 sessions; ranging markets
                                           are the hardest environment to trade
                                           directionally)

  Gate 2 — Flow confirmation:
    cvd_norm > 0.02 (effectively: net buy pressure must be slightly positive)
    Rationale: even a weak positive CVD confirms that buyers are present in the
    market. A strong signal_score with negative CVD often means the price
    moved but the money didn't follow — a false signal.

  Gate 3 — Positioning filter:
    funding_signal < 0.55 (raw funding rate roughly below +0.00055)
    Rationale: if longs are already heavily crowded (high positive funding),
    entering now means buying into a position that is already extended. The
    risk/reward is asymmetric to the downside.

  Additionally:
    - MEGA will not open a new position if it already holds 3 open positions
      (max_positions). Concentration risk beyond 3 becomes difficult to manage
      at MEGA's capital level.
    - MEGA will not open a new position if cash is below 25% of total portfolio
      value (40% in volatile regimes). This ensures there is always dry powder
      for opportunities and stops MEGA from becoming fully illiquid.

  THREAT MODE:
    When threatened (survival system has flagged MEGA), both thresholds ease:
    signal threshold drops by 0.08, CVD gate is relaxed to > 0. The rationale
    is that when threatened, holding cash passively is the fastest path to
    elimination — some action, even lower-conviction, is better than none.

PART 4 — POSITION SIZING
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Position size is determined by two compounding factors:

  1. Half-Kelly fraction
     Once a pair has at least 6 closed trades in history, MEGA uses the
     Half-Kelly formula:

       f_half = 0.5 × (b × p − q) / b

     Where:
       p = win rate on this specific pair (as a decimal)
       q = 1 − p (loss rate)
       b = avgWin / avgLoss (payoff ratio)

     Using half-Kelly (rather than full Kelly) is a deliberate risk-reduction
     choice. Full Kelly maximises long-run growth but produces very large
     drawdowns. Half-Kelly gives ~75% of the growth rate with significantly
     lower volatility of outcomes.

     If Kelly returns ≤ 0 (negative expectancy), no position is taken at all
     regardless of signal strength.

     If there is insufficient history (<6 trades on a pair), the base size
     fraction of 20% is used as a flat fallback.

     The Kelly fraction is capped at 2× the base size (40% of capital) to
     prevent runaway over-betting on a lucky streak.

  2. Conviction scalar
     The Kelly fraction is then multiplied by a conviction factor derived from
     the signal_score:

       conviction = clamp(|signal_score| / 0.5, 0.5, 1.0)

     A signal_score of 0.50 gives full conviction (1.0×). A signal near the
     entry threshold (e.g. 0.15) gives minimum conviction (0.5×). The idea is
     that a borderline signal should result in a smaller bet than a high-
     confidence signal, even if both clear the entry gate.

  The minimum tradeable amount is max(capital × 0.005, $1). Below this, MEGA
  holds rather than opening a position too small to be meaningful relative to
  fees.

PART 5 — EXIT LOGIC (SELL)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Each held position is evaluated against the exit thresholds of its own current
regime — not a session-wide regime. This means a trending_down position gets
the tighter 4% stop even if BTC is currently ranging. Exit checks are evaluated
in strict priority order. The first trigger that fires wins.

  Priority 1 — Hard stop-loss (immediate):
    Exit if unrealised P&L < −6% (−4% in trending_down regime).
    This is non-negotiable. No signal strength, no market context overrides it.
    The tighter stop in trending_down reflects that in a falling market,
    losers tend to keep falling; cutting sooner limits damage.
    Stop-loss also fires intra-candle via a separate lightweight scan (not just
    at candle close), so a fast move does not require waiting a full tick.

  Priority 2 — Soft exits (first qualifying position exits):
    Any of the following triggers a sell of the first qualifying position found:

    a) Momentum gone: signal_score < −0.12 (sell_signal threshold)
       The composite signal has turned clearly bearish. The thesis that drove
       the entry no longer holds.

    b) Selling pressure: cvd_norm < −0.25
       Despite the signal score, real money is leaving. CVD turning strongly
       negative means sellers are aggressively hitting bids — a direct threat
       to the position regardless of other indicators.

    c) Take-profit: unrealised P&L > +12% AND cvd_norm < 0
       Lock in gains when a meaningful profit target is reached AND flow is
       starting to turn. The CVD condition prevents premature exit on a strong
       trending move — MEGA will only take profit if flow is weakening.

    d) Deadweight: held ≥ 5 rounds AND |unrealised P&L| < 3%
       If a position has been held for at least 5 rounds (currently 75 minutes
       at 15m intervals) without moving meaningfully in either direction,
       capital is being wasted. Deadweight exits free up capacity for better
       setups. The 5-round minimum is itself a learnable parameter — if MEGA
       is exiting too many positions as deadweight before they develop, the
       pipeline will propose raising this threshold.

  THREAT MODE:
    When threatened, the sell_signal threshold eases by 0.08 (i.e. MEGA
    tolerates more negative signal before exiting, since it needs to stay
    active to accumulate trades for survival scoring).

PART 6 — SELF-EVOLUTION (the proposal pipeline)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

MEGA's config is not static. After each session, a post-session pipeline runs
automatically:

  1. export-session.js    Captures all closed trades, signals at entry, exit
                          reasons, regime context, and per-pair P&L.

  2. report-session.js    Generates per-regime win rates, average P&L,
                          stop-loss rates, deadweight rates, and signal
                          accuracy into a structured analysis file.

  3. compare-sessions.js  Builds trend arrays across all sessions into
                          trends.json (e.g. ranging win rates over last 9
                          sessions, MEGA deadweight exit rates, etc.)

  4. detect-changes.js    Applies rules against the trend data. Each rule
                          checks whether a pattern has persisted for at least
                          3 consecutive sessions before proposing a change.
                          Only ONE proposal is generated per session.

  Current rules:
    Rule 1: A/B/G combined regime win rate < 42% for 3 sessions
            → raise MEGA regime buy_signal threshold (+0.03)

    Rule 2: A/B/G combined regime avg PnL < −0.15% for 3 sessions
            → raise MEGA regime buy_signal threshold (+0.03)

    Rule 6: A/B/G combined regime win rate > 70% for 3 sessions
            → lower MEGA regime buy_signal threshold (−0.03, not below 0.05)

    Rule 7: A/B/G combined regime avg PnL > +0.50% for 3 sessions
            → lower MEGA regime buy_signal threshold (−0.03)

    Rule 8: A/B/G combined regime stop-loss exit rate > 50% for 3 sessions
            → raise MEGA regime sell_loss_pct (+1pp, max 12%)

    Rule 8b: A/B/G combined regime stop-loss exit rate < 15% for 3 sessions
            → lower MEGA regime sell_loss_pct (−1pp, floor 4%). The stop is
            wider than current conditions require; tightening recovers P&L
            on losing trades without increasing stop-out frequency.

    Rule 9: MEGA own deadweight exit rate > 40% overall for 3 sessions
            → raise strategy.deadweight_rounds_min (+2 rounds, max 15)

    Rule 9b: MEGA own deadweight exit rate < 15% overall for 3 sessions
            → lower strategy.deadweight_rounds_min (−2 rounds, floor 5).
            Positions are consistently moving before the timer fires —
            the minimum hold time has been raised above what conditions
            require and should be allowed to come back down.

  Rules 1, 2, 6, 7, 8 use A/B/G peer agents as market proxies rather than
  MEGA's own sparse data. MEGA's small capital ($500) means it trades far less
  frequently than the $1M agents, so 3 sessions of MEGA-only data may be 6–10
  trades — too thin for reliable statistics. A/B/G combined across the same
  regime provides a far more stable signal.

  Rule 9 uses MEGA's own exits because deadweight behaviour is agent-specific
  (driven by MEGA's capital size and the pairs it holds), not a market-wide
  regime condition.

  A proposed change is automatically applied at the end of the pipeline run.
  The TUI displays an informational overlay with the full justification and
  the before/after values; the operator dismisses it at their convenience
  — no approval step required. A backup of mega-config.json with an ISO
  timestamp is saved before every applied change. Rejected proposals (not
  applicable in auto-apply mode) would be suppressed for 3 sessions before
  re-evaluation; suppression logic remains in place for future use.

PART 7 — GAMMA MARKET-NEUTRAL EXTENSION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

GAMMA has been extended from a pure long-only Risk Manager into a market-
neutral sim agent. It now maintains a separate short capital pool alongside
its existing long book. This change does not affect ALPHA or BETA.

Purpose
───────
GAMMA is not modified to earn P&L on short trades. Its purpose remains
data generation for the self-evolution pipeline. By running both long and
short positions simultaneously, GAMMA produces regime-specific short
profitability data that the pipeline can use to propose short activation
on MEGA once statistical confidence is established.

The question GAMMA is answering: "In which regimes, with which signals,
would a short position have been profitable over the observed sessions?"

Architecture (Option A2 — separate capital pool)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
GAMMA maintains two distinct capital pools:

  Long pool:   $10,000  — existing capital, unchanged behaviour
  Short pool:  $2,000   — 20% of initial capital, dedicated to shorts

The two pools are completely isolated:
  - A loss on a short does not affect the long pool, and vice versa
  - GAMMA's survival score is computed from the long pool only
  - portfolioHistory (used for Sharpe/MaxDD metrics) tracks long capital only
  - The short pool is displayed separately in the TUI (GAMMA box only)

Short entry conditions (gammaDecide)
─────────────────────────────────────
All three gates must pass simultaneously:

  Gate 1 — Bearish signal:   signal_score  < −0.25
  Gate 2 — Selling flow:     cvd_norm      < −0.10  (sellers aggressively hitting bids)
  Gate 3 — Allowed regime:   regime ∈ {trending_down, volatile}

Additional constraints:
  - Maximum 1 short position open at any time
  - GAMMA will not short a pair it is currently long
  - Minimum $50 available in short pool required to open a position
  - Position size = 50% of available short pool (max $1,000)

Short entry — persistence gate (2-candle confirmation)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Before GAMMA enters a short, the same pair must qualify on two consecutive
candles. The flow is:

  Round N: bearish signal passes all gates → emit SHORT_CANDIDATE
           (stored in agent.shortCandidates[pair] = { score, round })
  Round N+1: same pair qualifies again AND candidate.round === N
           → emit SHORT (confirmed entry)

If the signal disappears between candles, the candidate is discarded.
Candidates older than 1 round are purged at the start of each decision.
This gate eliminates single-candle noise entries that immediately reverse
(the primary failure mode observed in early GAMMA short data).

Cover (exit) conditions — evaluated before long sell logic each tick
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Priority 1 — Stop-loss:    unrealised short loss > 8%   (price rose 8% from entry)
  Priority 2 — Take-profit:  unrealised short gain > 8%   (price fell 8% from entry)
  Priority 3 — Thesis faded: signal_score > dynCoverThr   (dynamic, see below)

Dynamic cover threshold (cover_alpha = 0.40)
─────────────────────────────────────────────
Instead of a fixed cover_signal = 0.00, the cover threshold is proportional
to the conviction at entry:

  dynCoverThr = entrySignalScore × cover_alpha
  e.g.: entry score −0.38 → dynCoverThr = −0.38 × 0.40 = −0.152

GAMMA will cover only when the score rises above −0.152, not at 0.00.
A stronger entry conviction requires the score to recover more before
covering — giving the trade more room while still exiting well before
the signal turns positive. The cover_alpha parameter (0.40) is a future
candidate for pipeline proposals once ≥ 15 closed short trades accumulate.

Stop-loss also fires intra-candle (same scan as long stop-losses) so a
fast upward move does not require waiting a full tick.

Short P&L mechanics
────────────────────
  Entry:  sell at BID (mid − half-spread)  — GAMMA "borrows" the asset notionally
  Cover:  buy back at ASK (mid + half-spread)
  P&L:    qty × (entryBidPrice − exitAskPrice) − coverFee
  Return: P&L / collateral × 100%

Entry and cover fees are standard Binance taker fee (0.10%), charged against
the short pool. The long pool capital_after and portfolioHistory are never
modified by short trades.

TUI display (GAMMA box only)
─────────────────────────────
A "Shorts" section appears between Holdings and the trading metrics:

  Shorts  pool:$1,400/$2,000
    ▼ BTC/USDT   $1,000  +3.2%  2r
    ▼ SOL/USDT   $0      (no open shorts)

  ▼ = short position marker (always red)
  P&L is shown green (price fell = profit) or red (price rose = loss)
  pool shows remaining short capital / pool maximum
  pool turns yellow at > 80% used, red when exhausted

Log display
────────────
SHORT entries appear in red (same colour as SELL). COVER entries show
the realised P&L and remaining pool balance:

  14:32:05 GAMMA SHORT BTC/USDT  $1,000 @ $84,200  fee $0.010  pool $1,000
    ↳ GAMMA short [trending_down] BTC: score=−0.38, cvd=−0.22...

  14:38:41 GAMMA COVER BTC/USDT  $1,068 @ $83,500  fee $0.008  +$68 (+6.8%)
    ↳ GAMMA cover BTC/USDT: short profit 8% reached (+6.8%).

Future proposal rules (not yet implemented — pending data accumulation)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
After GAMMA accumulates sufficient short history (minimum 3 sessions with
≥5 closed short trades each), the pipeline will add:

  Rule 10:  GAMMA short win rate < 40% for 3 sessions
            → raise |short_signal| threshold (tighter entry bar)

  Rule 10b: GAMMA short win rate > 65% for 3 sessions
            → lower |short_signal| threshold (more opportunities)

  Rule 11:  GAMMA short P&L persistently positive in trending_down
            for 3 sessions (win rate > 55%, avg return > +0.5%)
            → propose to MEGA: enable short in trending_down regime
              { field: strategy.allow_short, proposed: true,
                evidence: { win_rate, avg_return, n_trades } }

These rules are blocked until the evidence base is solid. No short
activation will be proposed to MEGA based on fewer than 15 closed
short trades.

          Triggered by MEGA's own deadweight exit rate of 66.7% over 3 sessions,
          well above the 40% threshold. Positions were being flagged as stale
          before they had time to develop. Raising to 7 rounds (105 minutes at
          15m intervals) gives entries more room to move before capital is freed.
