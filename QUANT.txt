PURPOSE OF THIS DOCUMENT
────────────────────────
This review analyses the MEGA Adaptive Synthesizer strategy as a formal
quantitative system. It does not describe what the strategy does (see
STRATEGY.txt for that). It examines whether the design choices are sound,
what must be true for the strategy to work, where it can break, and how to
test and improve it. The goal is to surface structural risks before they
appear in live P&L and to propose experiments that generate evidence rather
than opinions.

All section references correspond to STRATEGY.txt unless otherwise noted.


═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SECTION 1 — STRUCTURAL ASSUMPTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

These are the claims the strategy implicitly makes about the market. If any
of them fail consistently, the strategy's expected value degrades regardless
of how well it is implemented.

  A1 — SIGNAL STATIONARITY
  ─────────────────────────
  Assumption: The seven sub-signals have stable predictive relationships with
  forward returns. The same cvd_norm > 0.25 that predicted a +1.2% move in
  Q4 2025 will predict a similar move in Q2 2026.

  Risk: Crypto markets undergo structural regime shifts (e.g. ETF approval,
  exchange failures, regulatory shocks) that permanently alter signal
  distributions. A weight set fit on one market structure may be actively
  misleading in another.

  Current mitigation: None. Weights are fixed manually.

  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  A2 — REGIME COVERAGE IS EXHAUSTIVE
  ────────────────────────────────────
  Assumption: The four regimes (volatile, trending_up, trending_down,
  ranging) partition all possible market states and each partition is
  internally homogeneous enough that a single threshold set applies cleanly
  within it.

  Risk: In practice, regimes blend and transition. A pair moving from
  ranging to trending_up creates an intermediate state where the ranging
  threshold (0.34) is still active but momentum signals are already elevated.
  The strategy has no transition logic — it snaps between thresholds
  discretely.

  Additionally, "volatile" is not the same as "directionally volatile." A
  volatile uptrend and a volatile crash are both classified as volatile, but
  the appropriate response differs.

  Current mitigation: Intra-candle stop-loss scan provides partial protection
  during the volatile regime.

  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  A3 — CVD IS A RELIABLE PROXY FOR ORDER FLOW INTENT
  ────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Assumption: Net taker buy/sell volume over the last 20 bars reflects real
  directional intent by informed participants, not noise, wash trading, or
  automated hedging.

  Risk: CVD at short intervals (15m) on altcoin pairs is heavily influenced
  by a small number of large fills that may not be directionally informative.
  A single 500-BTC market buy can dominate 20 bars of CVD for a mid-cap pair
  while the underlying order book remains flat.

  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  A4 — FUNDING RATE IS A CROWDING SIGNAL, NOT A CARRY COST
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Assumption: A high positive funding rate (longs paying shorts) predicts
  mean-reversion or underperformance of long positions, not just elevated
  carry cost.

  Risk: In sustained trending markets, funding can remain elevated for
  extended periods while price continues upward. The contrarian interpretation
  works as a filter on entry timing, but can suppress entries during the
  highest-momentum phase of a genuine uptrend. The current threshold of 0.55
  (normalised) was set heuristically. It has not been backtested for optimal
  cutoff by regime.

  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  A5 — HISTORICAL WIN RATE AND PAYOFF RATIO ARE PREDICTIVE
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Assumption: The Half-Kelly calculation uses historical p (win rate) and b
  (avgWin / avgLoss) from prior trades on each pair. This assumes those
  parameters are stable — that the strategy's next N trades on BTC will
  resemble the last N.

  Risk: With the 6-trade minimum threshold, Kelly can activate after a run of
  6 lucky wins on a pair, producing an inflated fraction that will be
  corrected only after subsequent losses erode the estimate. The 2× cap
  (40% of capital) is a partial guard but does not address the cold-start
  estimation problem.

  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  A6 — PEER AGENTS (A/B/G) ARE VALID MARKET PROXIES FOR MEGA
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Assumption: A/B/G trade the same 15 pairs, so their per-regime win rates
  and P&L are representative of what MEGA would achieve in the same regime if
  it traded at greater frequency.

  Risk: A/B/G operate with $1M capital each. Market impact, fill quality, and
  position sizing at that scale differ materially from MEGA's $500 base.
  A ranging win rate of 40% for ALPHA at $1M capital does not guarantee 40%
  for MEGA at $500, particularly if MEGA is trading less liquid pairs where
  small sizes are filled at worse prices relative to signal quality. This
  assumption becomes more fragile if A/B/G ever diverge in archetype or
  signal set from MEGA.

  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  A8 — GAMMA SIM SHORT P&L GENERALISES TO MEGA FUTURES SHORTS
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Assumption: Short trades simulated by GAMMA against spot price data
  (using bid-ask spread + 0.10% taker fee as cost model) are a valid
  predictor of what MEGA would achieve executing equivalent positions
  on Binance Futures (fapi.binance.com).

  Risk: Several differences between GAMMA's sim and real Futures execution
  may invalidate the generalisation:
    (a) Funding cost: Futures shorts pay/receive funding every 8h. In a
        bearish market, funding is often negative (shorts receive it), which
        improves returns. GAMMA's sim does not model funding carry on open
        short positions.
    (b) Liquidation mechanics: Real Futures has liquidation risk at the
        margin limit. GAMMA's hardcoded 8% stop does not replicate this.
    (c) Basis risk: Futures price ≠ spot price. GAMMA shorts against spot
        mid; futures entry price can deviate meaningfully at high funding
        regimes or near expiry.
    (d) Leverage: GAMMA shorts are 1x (notional = collateral). Real Futures
        at 1x are equivalent, but the temptation to use leverage will distort
        the analogy if MEGA ever runs at > 1x.

  Current mitigation: Rule 11 (not yet active) requires ≥15 closed short
  trades and 3 consecutive positive sessions before proposing MEGA activation.
  The accumulated evidence reduces (but does not eliminate) the basis risk.

  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  A7 — THREAT MODE IMPROVES EXPECTED OUTCOMES UNDER THREAT
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Assumption: When MEGA is threatened, relaxing entry and exit thresholds
  (−0.08 on buy_signal, CVD gate → > 0) increases the expected number of
  surviving trades enough to offset the lower quality of those trades.

  Risk: Threat mode is a desperation heuristic, not a derived optimum. Lower
  thresholds mean more false positives, more stop-losses, and potentially
  faster capital erosion — the opposite of survival. The assumption that
  "any action is better than inaction" is a psychological argument, not a
  quantitative one.

SECTION 2 — FAILURE MODES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Failure modes are ordered by estimated severity × probability. Each mode
identifies the mechanism, the observable signature, and what distinguishes it
from normal underperformance.

  F1 — REGIME MISCLASSIFICATION DURING TRANSITIONS
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Mechanism: The regime classifier uses a 20-bar lookback. At the point of a
  true trend reversal (e.g. trending_up → trending_down), the classifier will
  remain in trending_up for several bars while price falls, applying the
  lower trending_up buy threshold (0.21) instead of the higher ranging (0.34)
  or trending_down (0.25) thresholds. This inverts the desired behaviour:
  the most dangerous moment for long entries gets the least restrictive gate.

  Observable signature: Cluster of stop-losses on a specific pair immediately
  following a local price peak, where regime was classified as trending_up at
  the time of entry.

  Distinguishing factor: The loss rate per trade during trending_up entries
  will be higher than the A/B/G proxy data for the same regime, because
  MEGA's entries were concentrated at trend reversals rather than trend
  continuations.

  Severity: HIGH. Every regime transition is a misclassification window.

  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  F2 — KELLY COLD-START OVERBET
  ──────────────────────────────
  Mechanism: Kelly activates at 6 trades per pair. If the first 6 trades on
  a pair are all winners (possible in a sustained trending period), the
  estimated win rate p approaches 1.0. Even half-Kelly with b = 1.5 would
  produce f = 0.5 × ((1.5 × 1.0 − 0.0) / 1.5) = 0.5. Combined with the 2×
  cap, MEGA could commit 40% of capital to one position immediately after the
  6-trade threshold is crossed on a pair that is coincidentally in a hot
  streak. If the streak is regime-driven rather than signal-driven, the
  first reversion trade will be oversized at the worst moment.

  Observable signature: A large loss on a specific pair within 1–3 trades of
  the Kelly activation point (trade count crossing 6).

  Severity: MEDIUM–HIGH. Capital is small ($500) so 40% per position is $200.
  The downside is bounded but material to survival.

  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  F3 — DEADWEIGHT EXITS AS SYSTEMATIC ALPHA LEAK
  ────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Mechanism: The deadweight exit (held ≥ N rounds, |P&L| < 3%) frees capital
  from stalled positions. If the underlying alpha is real but slow-developing,
  the exit removes positions just before they would have triggered the +12%
  take-profit. The position is then re-entered later (if signal recovers) at
  a higher cost basis, with fees paid twice.

  The rate of 66.7% deadweight exits over 3 sessions (which triggered v13)
  is itself ambiguous: it could mean the positions were genuinely worthless
  OR that the hold time is too short for the underlying signal to express.
  The fix (raising rounds_min from 5 to 7) increases hold time but does not
  answer the question of whether the signal has positive forward value at
  these positions.

  Observable signature: Pairs that are frequently deadweight-exited, then
  re-entered on the same bar or 1–2 bars later — the signal recovered
  immediately after exit.

  Severity: MEDIUM. Primarily a fee drag and opportunity cost issue rather
  than catastrophic loss.

  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  F4 — SIGNAL WEIGHT MISCALIBRATION (FUNDING DOMINANCE)
  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Mechanism: funding_signal carries 0.22 weight — the second highest signal
  weight. It is inverted, meaning high positive funding reduces the composite
  score. In a market where funding is structurally elevated (e.g. high
  open interest, many retail longs on leverage), this signal will persistently
  suppress composite scores across all pairs and all regimes, reducing entry
  frequency below the level the other six signals would support.

  This is not a signal-specific failure but a weight calibration failure:
  the 0.22 weight was selected by intuition, not by optimisation against
  realised returns.

  Observable signature: Long multi-session periods where composite scores
  consistently sit 0.1–0.2 below entry thresholds despite strong CVD and
  momentum signals. MEGA sitting in cash while A/B/G continue to trade.

  Severity: MEDIUM. Opportunity cost, not direct loss. Can be catastrophic
  for survival if MEGA runs out of trades to score.

  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  F5 — PROPOSAL PIPELINE LATENCY
  ────────────────────────────────
  Mechanism: The self-evolution pipeline requires a pattern to persist for
  3 consecutive sessions before proposing a change. With 15-minute ticks, a
  full session is approximately 96 ticks. Three sessions is roughly 8 hours
  of real trading. In a fast-moving market shift (e.g. sudden shift from
  trending to volatile across all pairs), the strategy may sustain 3 sessions
  of losses before any threshold adjustment is proposed — and then a further
  session before the change is live (proposals are now auto-applied at the
  end of the pipeline run, so there is no additional approval delay).

  Observable signature: A visible degradation in win rate beginning at
  session N, with the corrective proposal not appearing until session N+3,
  and the fix not applied until session N+4 or later.

  Severity: LOW–MEDIUM. The 3-session requirement is intentional to prevent
  overfitting to noise. But it creates an irreducible adaptation lag.

  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  F6 — SINGLE-PROPOSAL BOTTLENECK
  ─────────────────────────────────
  Mechanism: detect-changes.js generates at most ONE proposal per session.
  If multiple rules fire simultaneously (e.g. ranging win rate is below
  threshold AND trending_up stop-loss rate is elevated), only the highest-
  confidence rule is proposed. The other conditions go unaddressed that
  session and must compete in the next session. In a genuinely bad market,
  multiple parameters may need adjustment simultaneously.

  Observable signature: Proposal history showing the same rule repeatedly
  proposed across sessions while other metrics continue to deteriorate
  unaddressed.

  Severity: LOW in isolation, but compounds with F5 (latency) when multiple
  parameters are wrong at the same time.

  Note: Rules 8, 8b, 9, and 9b each target a different parameter or
  direction — they do not compete with each other for the same field in the
  same session, which reduces bottleneck pressure for those specific rules.

  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  F8 — GAMMA SHORT POOL DEPLETION (DATA BLACKOUT)
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Mechanism: GAMMA's short pool is $2,000. With a position size of 50%
  per trade ($1,000 max) and a stop-loss at 8%, the worst case per stop
  is −$80. After 25 consecutive maximum-size stop-losses, the pool is
  exhausted. Once exhausted, GAMMA can no longer open short positions —
  the pipeline loses its short data source entirely.

  The pool does not auto-replenish. It is restored only on GAMMA respawn
  (triggered by the long-side bankruptcy rule). A depleted pool can persist
  for many sessions, during which Rule 11 (propose shorts to MEGA) cannot
  fire even if conditions are favourable.

  Observable signature: GAMMA box in TUI shows `pool: $0/$2,000`.
  Short entry log entries cease. closedShorts[] accumulates no new data.

  Distinguishing factor: Not visible in MEGA's own performance — MEGA
  continues trading longs normally. The failure is in the data pipeline,
  not in live P&L, making it easy to miss.

  Severity: MEDIUM. Does not cause direct financial loss, but silences
  the short-opportunity signal at exactly the moments when bearish data
  would be most valuable (sustained trending_down markets).

  Mitigation: Consider adding a pool reset trigger: if GAMMA respawns
  for any reason, also reset shortCapital to the initial $2,000.

  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  F7 — THREAT MODE ACCELERATED RUIN
  ───────────────────────────────────
  Mechanism: When survival pressure activates threat mode, entry thresholds
  drop by 0.08. This increases trade frequency but at reduced signal quality.
  If the threat itself was caused by a bad market environment (e.g. all pairs
  in volatile regime), forcing trades in that environment at lower conviction
  will produce more stop-losses, depleting capital faster than passive waiting
  would have. Threat mode can accelerate the outcome it is trying to prevent.

  Observable signature: Threat mode activation followed by a sequence of
  stop-losses in rapid succession (≤ 3 rounds between stops). Net capital
  falls faster during threat mode than the preceding period.

  Severity: HIGH. The failure mode is not visible until it is too late to
  reverse. Capital erosion is irreversible in the current session context.

SECTION 3 — PARAMETER SENSITIVITY ANALYSIS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The parameters below have the highest estimated impact on outcome. Each is
assessed for its sensitivity range — the interval over which changes produce
meaningful shifts in expected P&L.

  buy_signal (base: 0.15)
  ────────────────────────
  Lower bound: ~0.10. Below this, almost every pair qualifies almost every
  tick, turning MEGA into a high-frequency momentum follower that will be
  stopped out repeatedly in ranging markets.

  Upper bound: ~0.45. Above this, MEGA rarely trades, particularly in ranging
  and volatile regimes where the override is already 0.30–0.34.

  Sensitivity: HIGH. A change of ±0.05 materially affects trade frequency,
  which in turn affects Kelly estimation quality and survival trade count.

  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  deadweight_rounds_min (current: 7)
  ────────────────────────────────────
  Lower bound: 3–4. Below this, nearly every position that doesn't immediately
  move is exited within one 15m candle interval, generating fees with no alpha.

  Upper bound: 15 (hard cap). At 15 rounds (225 minutes), positions are held
  for nearly 4 hours minimum. The strategy begins to resemble a swing position
  holder rather than an intraday system, which changes the expected correlation
  of its P&L with the signal inputs.

  Sensitivity: MEDIUM. Primarily affects fee drag and capital utilisation
  rather than per-trade P&L. The current value of 7 was derived from an
  empirical 66.7% exit rate, not from forward P&L optimisation.

  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  sell_loss_pct (base: 6%, trending_down: 4%)
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────
  Lower bound: 2–3%. Below this, normal price noise triggers stops on entries
  with correct directional thesis. The stop is hit before the trade has time
  to express.

  Upper bound: 10–12%. Beyond this, individual stop-losses begin to represent
  significant capital erosion (12% loss on a 20% position = 2.4% of capital
  per trade). With max_positions = 3, simultaneous stops at 12% would reduce
  capital by ~7.2% in a single round.

  Sensitivity: HIGH. The relationship between stop tightness and stop-loss
  rate is nonlinear. A stop that is too tight creates high stop-loss rates
  without reducing per-stop loss (since the trade never had room to develop).

  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  funding_signal weight (current: 0.22)
  ───────────────────────────────────────
  Impact: Because funding is the second-highest weight and is contrarian
  (high funding reduces score), it systematically compresses composite scores
  in high-interest environments. Reducing this weight to 0.10–0.12 would
  roughly add +0.07–0.10 to average composite scores, shifting borderline
  entries above threshold.

  Sensitivity: MEDIUM. The impact is directional bias rather than variance.
  The correct weight depends on whether the funding-as-crowding thesis holds
  empirically across observed sessions.

  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  short_signal (GAMMA, current: −0.25)
  ──────────────────────────────────────
  This threshold determines how bearish the composite signal must be before
  GAMMA considers a short entry. It mirrors buy_signal for longs.

  Lower bound (more negative, e.g. −0.40): Very few shorts triggered. Data
  accumulation is slow. Only the most extreme bearish signals qualify, which
  may under-represent moderate bearish regimes.

  Upper bound (less negative, e.g. −0.15): Too many shorts in ranging and
  mild trending_down regimes where the edge is unclear. Increases pool
  depletion risk without proportional improvement in data quality.

  Sensitivity: MEDIUM. The threshold directly controls short trade frequency
  and thus how fast Rule 11 evidence accumulates. It is adjustable via
  Rules 10/10b once sufficient data exists.

  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  short_stop_pct / short_profit_pct (GAMMA, both currently: 8%)
  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Symmetric 8% thresholds create a 1:1 payoff ratio before edge. With a
  win rate above 50%, expectancy is positive. The equality is intentional:
  there is no a priori reason to expect shorts to have an asymmetric payoff
  profile at this stage. These will be revised once empirical data exists.

  The 8% stop is hardcoded (not proposable) to prevent the pipeline from
  widening it beyond a safe bound before the short mechanics are validated.

  Sensitivity: HIGH for P&L, LOW for pool survival. Tightening to 5% reduces
  pool depletion risk but increases stop-out frequency. Loosening to 12%
  amplifies individual losses without necessarily improving the win rate.

  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  max_positions (current: 3)
  ───────────────────────────
  At 3 positions with 20% base sizing, full deployment is 60% of capital
  with 40% cash reserve. This is conservative.

  Increasing to 4 or 5 increases capital utilisation but amplifies correlated
  drawdowns — crypto pairs are highly correlated during market-wide sell-offs,
  so 5 positions in volatile conditions can stop-loss simultaneously.

  Sensitivity: LOW in normal conditions, HIGH in correlated crash scenarios.
  The current limit of 3 is appropriate given the absence of cross-pair
  correlation measurement in the entry logic.

SECTION 4 — VALIDATION TESTS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

These are concrete tests that can be run against existing session data to
validate or invalidate the key assumptions. Each test has a clear pass/fail
criterion.

  T1 — SIGNAL PREDICTIVE VALIDITY
  ─────────────────────────────────
  Question: Does a higher signal_score at entry actually correlate with better
  forward returns?

  Method: From session export data, group all closed trades into four bins by
  signal_score at entry: [threshold, threshold+0.10), [+0.10, +0.20),
  [+0.20, +0.30), [+0.30, 1.0]. Compute mean realised P&L per bin.

  Pass criterion: Mean P&L should be monotonically increasing across bins.
  A higher-conviction entry should produce a higher average return.

  Fail indicator: No monotonic relationship, or bins above 0.30 show lower
  mean P&L than the 0.10–0.20 bin. This would indicate the signal is not
  calibrated: the high-conviction scores are not corresponding to better
  outcomes.

  Data requirement: Minimum 50 closed trades with entry signal recorded.

  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  T2 — REGIME CLASSIFICATION ACCURACY
  ─────────────────────────────────────
  Question: Are positions entered under the "correct" regime for the market
  conditions that followed?

  Method: For each closed trade, record the regime at entry. Compare the
  price direction over the subsequent 5 bars against the classified regime.
  A trending_up regime should be followed by upward price movement > 50%
  of the time. A ranging regime should show no directional bias.

  Pass criterion:
    trending_up   → next-5-bar return > 0 in ≥ 55% of cases
    trending_down → next-5-bar return < 0 in ≥ 55% of cases
    ranging       → |next-5-bar return| < 1σ in ≥ 60% of cases
    volatile      → any direction in ≥ 70% of cases (high |return|)

  Fail indicator: trending_up regime followed by negative returns at the
  same rate as ranging regime — indicates the trend classifier is not
  separating market states meaningfully.

  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  T3 — KELLY COLD-START SIZING AUDIT
  ────────────────────────────────────
  Question: Is MEGA's position size at trade #6 per pair materially larger
  than at trade #5, and does trade #7+ perform worse than the Kelly estimate
  would predict?

  Method: For each pair, extract the sequence of trades and mark the index
  at which Kelly activates (trade 6). Compare the realised return distribution
  for trades 1–5 (flat 20% sizing) against trades 6+ (Kelly sizing).

  Pass criterion: Trades 6+ should have higher average P&L than trades 1–5,
  or at minimum no worse, since Kelly is sizing proportionally to estimated
  edge.

  Fail indicator: Trades 6+ show a lower win rate or lower average P&L than
  the flat-sizing period. This indicates the Kelly activation is occurring
  too early, before estimates are stable.

  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  T4 — DEADWEIGHT EXIT COUNTERFACTUAL
  ─────────────────────────────────────
  Question: Do positions exited as deadweight have positive or negative
  forward P&L if held for an additional 3–5 rounds?

  Method: For each deadweight exit, record the price at exit and the price
  3, 5, and 7 rounds later. Compute hypothetical P&L had the position been
  held.

  Pass criterion: Mean hypothetical P&L at +3 rounds should be near zero
  (meaning deadweight exits are genuinely capturing stalled positions, not
  cutting early winners).

  Fail indicator: Mean hypothetical P&L at +5 rounds is > +1.5%. This
  indicates that deadweight exits are systematically cutting positions just
  before they develop, and the hold time should be raised further.

  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  T5 — CVD GATE CONTRIBUTION ANALYSIS
  ─────────────────────────────────────
  Question: Is the cvd_norm > 0.02 entry gate adding positive value, or is
  it simply reducing trade frequency without improving the quality of trades
  that do occur?

  Method: From DECISION-type events where signal_score passes Gate 1 and
  Gate 3 but fails Gate 2 (CVD), record the subsequent price move for that
  pair over the next 5 bars. Compare this against trades that passed all
  three gates.

  Pass criterion: The mean forward return for Gate-2-rejected signals should
  be lower than for Gate-2-passed trades. The gate is earning its keep.

  Fail indicator: Gate-2-rejected signals have similar or higher forward
  returns than accepted trades. The CVD gate is filtering out good trades
  and is miscalibrated.

  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  T6 — THREAT MODE SURVIVAL AUDIT
  ─────────────────────────────────
  Question: Does threat mode activation actually improve MEGA's survival
  outcome in the sessions where it fired?

  Method: For each session in which threat mode activated, record: (a) the
  win rate on trades taken during threat mode vs. trades outside threat mode
  in the same session, and (b) whether MEGA survived the session.

  Pass criterion: Threat-mode trade win rate ≥ normal-mode win rate, OR
  survival rate in sessions where threat mode fired ≥ 70%.

  Fail indicator: Threat-mode trades have a lower win rate than normal-mode
  trades in the same session, AND MEGA elimination rate is elevated in
  threat-mode sessions. This is the signature of F7 (accelerated ruin).

  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  T7 — PEER AGENT PROXY VALIDITY
  ────────────────────────────────
  Question: Are A/B/G per-regime win rates correlated with MEGA's own
  per-regime win rates in the same sessions?

  Method: For sessions where MEGA has ≥ 3 trades in a given regime, compute
  the Pearson correlation between A/B/G combined regime win rate and MEGA's
  own regime win rate across all available sessions.

  Pass criterion: Correlation ≥ 0.6 (moderate positive relationship).

  Fail indicator: Correlation < 0.3 or negative. This would invalidate the
  core assumption behind Rules 1, 2, 6, 7, and 8 — that A/B/G behaviour
  is a valid proxy for MEGA. All five rules would need to be rebuilt using
  MEGA-only data or a different proxy.

SECTION 5 — RECOMMENDED EXPERIMENTS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Experiments are designed to produce evidence about specific design choices.
Each is an A/B test or incremental change that can be evaluated within
5–10 sessions. They are ordered by estimated information gain relative to
implementation cost.

  E1 — SIGNAL WEIGHT OPTIMISATION (HIGH PRIORITY)
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Hypothesis: The current signal weights (set manually) are not optimal.
  In particular, funding_signal at 0.22 may be suppressing composite scores
  in high-funding environments. A data-derived weight set would outperform
  the manual one.

  Method: After accumulating ≥ 200 closed trades with full signal vectors
  at entry, run a logistic regression of (win/loss outcome) on the seven
  normalised sub-signal values at entry. The resulting coefficients, when
  renormalised to sum to 1.0, give a data-derived weight estimate. Compare
  the implied trade selection against the current weight set.

  Success metric: The regression-derived weights produce a higher in-sample
  AUC than a naive equal-weight baseline. If funding's implied coefficient
  is < 0.10, reduce its weight in the live config.

  Implementation cost: LOW. Requires only a Python/R script against the
  session export data.

  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  E2 — REGIME HYSTERESIS BUFFER (HIGH PRIORITY)
  ───────────────────────────────────────────────
  Hypothesis: Discrete regime snapping at transition points (F1) causes
  systematic entry-at-reversal. Adding a hysteresis buffer — requiring a
  regime to be stable for N bars before its thresholds apply — would reduce
  transition-window entries.

  Method: Implement an optional `regime_min_bars` parameter (e.g. 3 bars).
  A regime only becomes "active" if it has held for 3 consecutive bars.
  During the transition window, use the previous confirmed regime's
  thresholds, or apply the most conservative threshold across both regimes.

  Run 10 sessions with `regime_min_bars = 3` vs. the current behaviour
  (immediate snap). Compare stop-loss rate during regime transition events.

  Success metric: Stop-loss rate within 3 bars of a regime change drops by
  ≥ 20% relative to the control period.

  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  E3 — KELLY WARM-UP RAMP (MEDIUM PRIORITY)
  ───────────────────────────────────────────
  Hypothesis: The 6-trade Kelly activation threshold produces cold-start
  overbet risk (F2). A gradual ramp — blending flat size and Kelly size
  proportionally as the trade count grows — would reduce this risk while
  still transitioning to Kelly-based sizing.

  Method:
    trade_count < 6:     size = flat_size (20%)
    trade_count 6–11:    size = flat_size × (1 − α) + kelly_size × α
                         where α = (count − 6) / 6, linearly 0 → 1
    trade_count ≥ 12:    size = kelly_size (fully Kelly)

  Run 5 sessions with the ramp active and compare maximum per-session
  drawdown vs. the control.

  Success metric: Maximum single-trade loss as a fraction of capital at the
  time of trade decreases by ≥ 15% in the first 12 trades per pair.

  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  E4 — FUNDING WEIGHT REDUCTION TEST (MEDIUM PRIORITY)
  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Hypothesis: Reducing funding_signal weight from 0.22 to 0.12 will increase
  trade frequency without materially degrading per-trade P&L. The freed
  weight should be redistributed proportionally to cvd_norm and momentum_1h.

  Proposed new weights: cvd_norm → 0.29, momentum_1h → 0.21,
  funding_signal → 0.12, others unchanged.

  Method: Run 5 sessions with the modified weights. Compare:
    - Trades per session
    - Win rate (should not decrease significantly)
    - Average P&L per trade (should not decrease significantly)
    - Stop-loss rate (should not increase significantly)

  Success metric: Trades per session increases by ≥ 20% with win rate
  degradation < 5pp. This would indicate that funding was suppressing valid
  entries rather than filtering bad ones.

  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  E5 — DEADWEIGHT HOLD-COST MEASUREMENT (MEDIUM PRIORITY)
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Hypothesis: The current deadweight threshold (|P&L| < 3%) is too wide.
  Positions with +2% unrealised gain are being treated the same as positions
  at −2%. A directional split — exit sooner if unrealised P&L is negative,
  hold longer if it is flat-to-positive — would improve capital utilisation.

  Proposed change: Split deadweight into two cases:
    Deadweight-negative:  held ≥ 5 rounds AND unrealised P&L ∈ (−3%, 0%)
    Deadweight-flat:      held ≥ 7 rounds AND unrealised P&L ∈ [0%, +3%)

  The negative case exits sooner (cuts losers), the positive case holds
  longer (gives small winners time to develop).

  Run T4 (counterfactual analysis) on historical exits to estimate the
  P&L improvement before implementing in the live config.

  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  E6 — THREAT MODE THRESHOLD AUDIT (LOW PRIORITY, HIGH RISK)
  ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Hypothesis: The −0.08 signal threshold relaxation in threat mode is
  arbitrary. A less aggressive relaxation (−0.04) might produce enough trades
  to satisfy survival scoring while reducing the quality-degradation risk.

  Method: In simulation, replay sessions where threat mode activated and
  test three threat thresholds: −0.04, −0.08 (current), −0.12. Compare
  trade count, win rate, and final capital at session end.

  Note: This experiment should be run on historical data before any live
  config change. The risk of getting this wrong is high — threat mode fires
  exactly when MEGA is most vulnerable, and the wrong threshold degrades the
  only chance of recovery.

  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  E8 — GAMMA SHORT REGIME PROFITABILITY BASELINE (HIGH PRIORITY)
  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Hypothesis: GAMMA's short positions will show a materially different
  win rate across regimes. trending_down should produce the highest short
  win rate; trending_up should produce the lowest (or never trigger, given
  the regime gate). This baseline is prerequisite data for Rule 11.

  Method: After 5–10 sessions of GAMMA short activity, aggregate
  closedShorts[] by regime at entry. Compute per-regime:
    - short win rate (return_pct > 0)
    - average return_pct
    - max drawdown of the short pool (as a parallel metric to the long MaxDD)

  Pass criterion:
    trending_down short win rate > 55% with avg return > +0.3%
    → sufficient evidence to propose Rule 11 evaluation

  Fail indicator:
    trending_down short win rate < 45% OR avg return < 0
    → either the regime gate needs tightening or the signal thresholds
    are not capturing genuine bearish setups. Revisit short_signal and
    short_cvd_max before activating MEGA short proposals.

  Data requirement: ≥ 15 closed short trades in trending_down regime.

  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  E7 — MULTI-PROPOSAL PER SESSION (LOW PRIORITY)
  ────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Hypothesis: The single-proposal-per-session constraint causes excessive
  adaptation lag when multiple parameters are simultaneously out of range
  (F6). Allowing up to 2 proposals per session when more than one rule
  fires, ordered by severity, would shorten the recovery window.

  Design consideration: With auto-apply, there is no operator review gate.
  Showing 2 proposals simultaneously (ordered by priority score) does not
  increase systemic risk; both would be auto-applied in sequence. The
  suppression logic (3 sessions after a change) should apply independently
  per rule, not globally.

  Implementation cost: LOW — change is in detect-changes.js only (TUI
  overlay is already informational).

SECTION 6 — OPEN QUESTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

These are unresolved design questions that do not have a clean answer yet.
They are recorded here to prevent them from being treated as resolved.

  Q1: What is the correct metric for "survival" in the scoring system?
      The current threat/survival system operates on undocumented criteria.
      If the scoring function rewards trade frequency over P&L quality, it
      creates a direct conflict with MEGA's low-frequency, high-conviction
      design. The two objectives need explicit reconciliation.

  Q2: How should regime multipliers interact with the conviction scalar?
      Currently the regime multiplier is applied to the raw signal_score
      before entry gating, while conviction is calculated after. This means
      a borderline signal in a trending market could pass the gate but receive
      a 0.5× conviction scalar, producing a position too small to be
      meaningful. Should the conviction floor be regime-adjusted as well?

  Q3: Is per-pair Kelly appropriate with only 15 pairs?
      With 15 pairs and a minimum of 6 trades each, full Kelly activation
      requires 90 trade instances, distributed unevenly across pairs. Some
      pairs will be in flat-size mode for many sessions while others have
      fully active Kelly. Should Kelly be estimated at the portfolio level
      (all pairs combined) rather than per pair, to achieve statistical
      significance sooner?

  Q4: What is the intended relationship between sell_profit_pct and
      regime? Currently take-profit at +12% is regime-invariant. In a
      trending_up market, +12% may be premature (the trend can continue
      much further). In a ranging market, +12% may be unreachable and the
      take-profit trigger never fires. Should take-profit targets also carry
      regime overrides?

  Q6: At what point does GAMMA's short data become a liability instead
      of a benefit? If GAMMA consistently loses on shorts (pool depletes
      repeatedly), the pipeline will never trigger Rule 11 — but the losses
      on the short pool do reduce GAMMA's total capital slightly via fees,
      which over many sessions may marginally affect its survival position
      relative to ALPHA and BETA. The threshold at which short activity
      should be disabled (short win rate persistently < 35% over 5+ sessions)
      is not currently defined. This needs a floor rule analogous to Rule 10.

  Q5: Rules 1/2 (raise buy_signal) vs. 6/7 (lower buy_signal) remain
      asymmetric: the raise conditions (win rate < 42%, avg PnL < −0.15%)
      are easier to trigger than the lower conditions (win rate > 70%, avg
      PnL > +0.50%). This creates a slow upward drift in buy_signal thresholds
      over time unless conditions are consistently strong. Rules 8/8b and
      9/9b are now symmetric (same session count, same step size, mirrored
      thresholds), so the ratchet concern is resolved for sell_loss_pct and
      deadweight_rounds_min. The asymmetry in buy_signal remains intentional
      but its long-run effect on trade frequency should be monitored.

SECTION 7 — SUMMARY RISK SCORECARD

  COMPONENT                      DESIGN QUALITY    EMPIRICAL VALIDATION
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Overall: The mechanical structure of the strategy is sound. The primary
  gaps are in empirical validation of signal weights, the threat mode
  logic, and the regime classifier at transition points. These are also
  the three areas with the highest potential impact on outcomes. They should
  be addressed in approximately the priority order described in Section 5.

  The GAMMA short book (v14) adds a data collection layer for future MEGA
  short activation. It is architecturally isolated (separate capital pool,
  no survival score impact) and gated behind Rule 11 evidence requirements,
  limiting the risk to GAMMA's $2,000 pool only. Empirical validation
  (E8) should begin as soon as 15 closed short trades are available.
